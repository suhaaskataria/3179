The Nobel Prize is one of the most coveted awards in academic research. Since it's beginning in 1901, there have been nearly a thousand talented individuals that have become Nobel Prize Laureates. This visualisation will explore the Nobel Prize and its Laureates in further detail.

The map on the left and chart below show the sheer dominance of the United States of America, with ovewr 250 Nobel Prize Laureates coming from its lands. The chart below shows the top 4 countries, and further highlights the US' impressive lead.

Let's discover the categories

There are ?6 categories in which you can win the Nobel Prize. As shown in the bar chart to the left, Physiology or Medicine has been the category with the most Laureates, at 169 prizes awarded.

With the prestigious award, the recipient is given Award Money. The visualisation to the right displays the increase in Award Money over the past 50 years, from a point in 1969 to 2019. Furthermore, this chart also makes it clear that all categories are paid equally.

Delving into the demographics of Nobel Prize Laureate, we can see the average ages that individuals win the award. Physics has the youngest award winners, with a median of 57.

Continuing the pursuit of understanding the demographics, we can visualise the winners by gender. The area chart on the right hand side shows the number of winners per gender over the past century. (it is interactive, you can highlight a region)

The pie chart shows the proportion of winners, highlighting that historically a significantly smaller number of females have been awarded the Nobel Prize.
